## 2024-05-18 - Avoid expanding any() generators to for loops
**Learning:** While replacing `any()` with generator expressions with explicit `for` loops provides a measurable micro-optimization in Python by avoiding generator setup overhead, it is considered an unacceptable sacrifice of code readability and Pythonic conventions unless profiling proves it is a massive bottleneck in a tight loop. It violates the principle of not sacrificing readability for micro-optimizations.
**Action:** Do not expand `any()` generators into `for` loops. Seek alternative optimizations that maintain or improve readability, such as using `join(split())` instead of `re.sub` for whitespace collapsing, or pre-compiling regexes.
